670-700 : Pandya Arikesari Parankusa Maravarman rules in Madurai

The period between 670 and 700 CE in Tamil Nadu, during the reign of Pandya Arikesari Parankusa Maravarman, was marked by various significant developments in the region. Here's an overview:

Pandya Dynasty:

The Pandya dynasty continued to be one of the major ruling families in Tamil Nadu during this time. They held sway over parts of the southern region, with their power center likely located in Madurai, a city known for its cultural and political significance.

Rule of Arikesari Parankusa Maravarman:

Arikesari Parankusa Maravarman was a notable ruler of the Pandya dynasty who is said to have ruled from Madurai during the period of 670-700 CE. His reign would have involved managing the affairs of the kingdom, maintaining political stability, and possibly engaging in military campaigns to expand or defend the territory.

Madurai as a Cultural Hub:

Madurai continued to be a vibrant center of culture, art, and learning during this period. The city was renowned for its temples, palaces, and bustling markets. It attracted poets, scholars, and merchants from across South India and beyond, contributing to its reputation as a center of intellectual and commercial activity.

Religious Developments:

The period saw the continued patronage of Hinduism by the rulers of Tamil Nadu. Temples and religious institutions flourished, with Madurai being home to several important Hindu temples dedicated to deities such as Meenakshi Amman and Sundareswarar. Religious festivals and rituals played a central role in the lives of the people.

Interactions with Other Dynasties:

The Pandya dynasty would have had interactions with neighboring dynasties such as the Cholas, Cheras, and Pallavas. These interactions could have involved diplomatic alliances, trade agreements, and occasional conflicts over territorial control.

Economic Prosperity:

Tamil Nadu's strategic location on the east coast of India made it an important center for trade and commerce. Ports such as Kaveripattinam (modern-day Puhar) facilitated maritime trade with other regions, including Southeast Asia and the Middle East. The region's agricultural wealth and commercial activities contributed to its economic prosperity.

Sangam Literature:

Although the Sangam Age had passed, the legacy of Sangam literature continued to influence Tamil society and culture. The poetic works composed during the Sangam period were revered and studied by scholars, contributing to the preservation of Tamil literary heritage.

Overall, the period of 670-700 CE in Tamil Nadu under the rule of Pandya Arikesari Parankusa Maravarman was characterized by political stability, cultural vibrancy, and economic prosperity. The region continued to play a significant role in shaping the history and culture of South India during this time.
