200 BCE-200 CE : Sangam age during which books of Sangam Literature was created

• The Sangam Age, spanning roughly from 200 BCE to 200 CE, is a significant period in the history of Tamil Nadu and southern India.
• It is named after the Sangam academies, which were gatherings of Tamil poets and scholars held during this time.
• The Sangam Age is renowned for the creation of Sangam literature, a collection of Tamil poetic works that provide valuable insights into the society, culture, and politics of ancient Tamil Nadu.

Here are some key aspects of the Sangam Age and Sangam literature:

• Sangam Literature:

• Sangam literature comprises poems, epics, and other literary works composed by various poets and scholars who were part of the Sangam academies.
• These works are classified into two main categories: "Ettuthokai" (Eight Anthologies) and "Pattupattu" (Ten Idylls).
•The poems cover a wide range of themes, including love, war, nature, ethics, and governance.

• Themes and Subjects:

• Sangam literature reflects the diverse facets of ancient Tamil society.
• The poems celebrate the beauty of nature, depict the lives of ordinary people, narrate heroic deeds of kings and warriors, and explore the intricacies of love and relationships.
• They also provide valuable historical information about the political landscape of ancient Tamil Nadu, including the existence of different kingdoms and the interactions between them.

• Poetic Style and Language:

• Sangam poetry is characterized by its elegance, imagery, and rich symbolism.
• The poets employed various literary devices such as metaphor, simile, alliteration, and wordplay to enhance the beauty and depth of their verses.
• The language of Sangam literature, known as "Classical Tamil," is considered the precursor to modern Tamil and is revered for its purity and sophistication.

• Social and Cultural Context:

• The Sangam Age was marked by the flourishing of trade, agriculture, and urbanization in Tamil Nadu.
• The society was organized into distinct social strata, including kings, nobles, merchants, farmers, and artisans.
• Sangam literature provides valuable insights into the customs, rituals, beliefs, and values of ancient Tamil society, offering a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage.

• Historical Significance:

• Sangam literature serves as an important historical source for understanding the early history of Tamil Nadu.
• The poems mention several ancient Tamil kingdoms, such as the Cholas, Cheras, and Pandyas, and provide details about their rulers, capitals, wars, and alliances.
• They also contain references to trade contacts with other regions, including Rome and Southeast Asia, highlighting the interconnectedness of ancient Tamil Nadu with the wider world.
